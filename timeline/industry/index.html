I wanted to take my API industry timeline, or at least the last 20+ years, and look at it differently. I wanted to generalize the digital resources that these companies were providing. Rather than focusing solely on moments in time, I wanted to consider how the accumulation of resources has enabled new possibilities. Understanding the regulation of earlier industries will inform the regulation of APIs and how we are using digital resources across applications and system integrations. While there will be some patterns of regulation that won't replay in the modern era, there are also some common patterns and anti-patterns that will shape how government views the tech sector, as well as how common people fear or embrace digital resources as part of their lives. I'll keep layering on my learnings from across other industries until I have a clearer picture of what might unfold when it comes to the regulation of APIs.
